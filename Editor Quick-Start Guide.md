## 1. Logging In

1. Open your web browser and go to:
   **rotaryclubely.co.uk/admin**
2. Click **"Log in with email"** and enter the email address you were invited
   with.
3. Check your inbox for a magic link email from Sanity. Click the link inside
   it. You are now logged in.

> **Tip:** Bookmark the admin URL so you can find it easily next time.
> You will not need a password — Sanity sends a fresh login link each time.

---

## 2. The Dashboard — Finding Your Way Around

When you log in you will see the **Sanity Studio** — this is your editing
workspace. Select Structure, then on the left-hand side you will see a menu with these sections:

| Section | What it contains |
|---|---|
| **Events** | Club events — Aquafest, Fireworks, community activities, etc. |
| **Latest** | News posts and announcements |
| **Our Impact** | Charitable contributions and stories |
| **Our Causes** | Causes and missions the club supports |
| **Pages** | Static pages such as About |
| **Members** | Club member and leadership profiles |
| **Sponsors** | Club sponsors and partners |
| **Settings** | Global site settings — meeting times, contact details, social links |

Click any section to see the entries inside it. Click an entry to open and
edit it. Click the pencil icon or **"Create new"** button to add a new one.

## 3. Creating an Event

Events appear on the **Events** page and — if featured — on the homepage.

1. In the left menu click **Events**, then click **"Create new"**.
2. Fill in the fields:

   **Title** _(required)_
   The full event name, e.g. "Ely Aquafest 2026" or "Christmas Social Evening".

   **Date Start** _(required)_
   The date and time the event begins. Click the field and use the date/time
   picker.

   **Date End** _(optional)_
   Only needed for multi-day events. Leave blank for single-day events.

   **Location** _(optional)_
   The venue name or area, e.g. "Riverside, Ely" or "The Maltings".

   **Category** _(required)_
   Choose from: Aquafest, Fireworks, Community, Social, Fundraising, or Other.
   This is used for filtering on the Events page.

   **Description** _(required)_
   The full event description. Use the formatting toolbar to add headings,
   bold text, bullet points, and links.

   **Hero Image** _(required)_
   The main image for this event. See **Section 6** for image upload
   instructions.

   **Gallery** _(optional)_
   Add extra photos. These appear in a gallery grid on the event page.
   See **Section 6** for upload instructions.

   **External URL** _(optional)_
   If the event has its own website (e.g. the Aquafest site), paste the full
   URL here. A "Visit Event Website" button will appear on the event page.

   **Featured** _(optional)_
   Tick this to show the event on the homepage. See **Section 8**.

3. When you are happy, click **"Publish"** in the bottom bar.
   The live site will update within a few minutes.

## 4. Creating a News Post

News posts appear on the **News** page and — if pinned — on the homepage.

1. In the left menu click **Latest**, then click **"Create new"**.
2. Fill in the fields:

   **Title** _(required)_
   A clear, descriptive headline, e.g. "Club Raises £3,000 at Annual Auction".

   **Date** _(required)_
   The publication date. This controls the order posts appear in.

   **Body** _(required)_
   The full text of the announcement. Use the formatting toolbar for headings,
   bold text, bullet points, and links.

   **Image** _(optional)_
   An optional photo to accompany the post.

   **Pinned** _(optional)_
   Tick this to keep the post at the top of the News page and show it on
   the homepage. See **Section 8**.

3. Click **"Publish"** when ready.

---

## 5. Creating a Charitable Contribution

Contribution stories appear on the **Our Impact** page.

1. In the left menu click **Our Impact**, then click **"Create new"**.
2. Fill in the fields:

   **Recipient** _(required)_
   The name of the charity, organisation, or cause that received support,
   e.g. "Ely Foodbank" or "East Anglian Air Ambulance".

   **Date** _(required)_
   When the contribution was made.

   **Amount** _(optional)_
   The financial value or description of support, e.g. "£2,500" or
   "50 volunteer hours". This appears as a badge on the story card.

   **Summary** _(required)_
   A single sentence describing what was given and why, e.g.
   "A donation of £2,500 to support the purchase of specialist equipment."
   This appears on listing pages and in search results.

   **Description** _(required)_
   The full story — who was helped, how the money was raised, what difference
   it made. Write as much as you like here.

   **Image** _(optional)_
   A photo related to the contribution — a handover photo works well here.

3. Click **"Publish"** when ready.

## 6. Adding and Cropping Images

Good images make a big difference to how the site looks. Here is how to
upload and prepare them.

### Uploading an image

1. Click the image field (it will say **"Upload image"** or show an existing
   image with an **"Edit"** button).
2. Either:
   - Click **"Upload"** and choose a file from your computer, or
   - Drag and drop an image file directly onto the field.
3. Wait for the upload to finish — you will see a preview of your image.

### Recommended image sizes

| Image type | Recommended size | Aspect ratio |
|---|---|---|
| Event hero image | 1600 × 900px minimum | 16:9 |
| Gallery images | 1200 × 800px minimum | Any |
| News post image | 1200 × 800px minimum | Any |
| Contribution image | 1200 × 800px minimum | Any |

Your photos do not need to be exactly these sizes — the site will
automatically resize and optimise them. These are minimums for good quality.

### Setting the hotspot (crop focus point)

After uploading, click the **"Edit"** button on the image. You will see two
tools:

- **Hotspot:** Click on the most important part of the image (e.g. a person's
  face, or the centre of a scene). The site will keep this area visible when
  the image is cropped to different shapes on different devices.
- **Crop:** Drag the handles to remove unwanted edges from the image.

Click **"Done"** when finished. This step is optional but recommended,
especially for hero images.

### Alt text

After uploading, you will see a field labelled **"Alt text"**. This is a
short description of what is in the image, e.g. "Rotary members handing a
cheque to Ely Foodbank volunteers". Alt text is important for accessibility
and search engines. Please always fill it in.

## 7. Using the Draft, Preview and Publish Workflow

You do not have to publish immediately. You can save your work as a draft and
come back to it later.

### Saving a draft

As you type, Sanity saves your work automatically. You will see
**"Edited"** in the top bar. Your changes are saved but not yet live on
the website.

### Previewing before publishing

To see how your content will look on the live site before publishing:

1. Click the **eye icon** (👁) in the top-right area of the editor.
2. A preview panel will open on the right showing a live rendering of the page.
3. Make any adjustments, then close the preview when you are done.

> **Note:** If you do not see the preview panel, your administrator can
> enable this feature.

### Publishing

When you are happy with your content:

1. Click the green **"Publish"** button at the bottom of the screen.
2. The live site will update within a few minutes.

You will see the status change from **"Edited"** to **"Published"**.

### Unpublishing

To take a published entry off the live site without deleting it:

1. Click the three-dot menu (**⋯**) at the top right of the editor.
2. Choose **"Unpublish"**.
3. The entry is now a draft again and will not appear on the live site.

## 8. Pinning and Featuring Content

### Pinning a news post

Pinned news posts appear at the **top of the News page** with a gold accent
border, and in the **Latest** section on the homepage.

To pin a post: open it, scroll to the **Pinned** field, and tick the checkbox.
Then publish.

> Keep the number of pinned posts to **1 or 2** at most. Too many pinned
> posts dilutes the effect.

### Featuring an event

Featured events appear in the **Upcoming Events** section on the homepage.

To feature an event: open it, scroll to the **Featured** field, and tick
the checkbox. Then publish.

> The homepage shows the next **3 upcoming events** automatically. Use
> Featured to prioritise which events appear there if you have many
> upcoming events at once.

## 9. Editing Site Settings

Site Settings control global information that appears across the whole site —
meeting details, contact information, and social media links.

1. In the left menu click **Settings**.
2. You will see a single document (there is only ever one). Click it to open.
3. Update any fields as needed:

   | Field | What it controls |
   |---|---|
   | Club Name | Appears in the site header and footer |
   | Meeting Day | e.g. "Every Thursday" |
   | Meeting Time | e.g. "8:00 PM" |
   | Meeting Location | e.g. "The City of Ely Bowls Club" |
   | Contact Email | The email address shown on the Contact page |
   | Phone | Optional contact number |
   | Social Links | Facebook, Instagram, and other social media URLs |
   | Footer Text | Additional text in the site footer |

4. Click **"Publish"** when done.

> **Important:** Changes to Site Settings affect the whole site. Double-check
> spelling and details carefully before publishing.

## 10. Editing an Existing Entry

1. Click the relevant section in the left menu (e.g. **Events**).
2. Find the entry you want to edit. You can scroll the list or use the
   search bar at the top.
3. Click the entry to open it.
4. Make your changes.
5. Click **"Publish"** to save your changes to the live site.

> **Version history:** Sanity keeps a full history of every change. If you
> make a mistake, click the **clock icon** (🕐) at the top right of the
> editor to browse previous versions and restore an earlier one.

---

## 11. Deleting an Entry

> **Warning:** Deleting is permanent. There is no recycle bin. If in doubt,
> unpublish the entry instead of deleting it — it will be hidden from the
> site but kept in the CMS.

To delete an entry:

1. Open the entry you want to delete.
2. Click the three-dot menu (**⋯**) at the top right.
3. Choose **"Delete"**.
4. Confirm the deletion when prompted.

---

## 12. If Something Goes Wrong

**The site has not updated after I published.**
The site usually updates within 1–2 minutes. If it takes longer than
5 minutes, try doing a hard refresh in your browser (Ctrl + Shift + R on
Windows, Cmd + Shift + R on Mac). If it still has not updated, contact
your site administrator.

**I accidentally published something and need to remove it quickly.**
Open the entry in Sanity, click the three-dot menu (**⋯**), and choose
**"Unpublish"**. The entry will be removed from the live site within
minutes while staying safely in the CMS.

**I uploaded the wrong image.**
Click **"Edit"** on the image field, then click **"Remove"** to clear it.
You can then upload the correct image.

**I cannot log in.**
Make sure you are using the same email address you were originally invited
with. Check your spam folder for the login email. If you are still stuck,
contact your site administrator to resend the invitation.

**I made a mistake and want to undo it.**
Click the clock icon (🕐) at the top right of the editor to open version
history. You can browse and restore any previous version of the entry.

**Something looks wrong on the live site but looks fine in the CMS.**
Take a screenshot and send it to your site administrator with a description
of what page it is on and what the problem looks like.
